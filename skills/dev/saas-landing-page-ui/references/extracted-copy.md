# Extracted Copy Notes from the Figma Reference

Source Figma file: `4 levels` (`yzPbFcv1lNAiIbqFh5P1aX`)

This file captures representative text patterns from the reference design. Use these as examples of copy direction and hierarchy, not as copy to paste into unrelated products.

## Level 1 Pattern: Descriptive and Generic

Representative examples:

- "Track your links with Linkd for better results."
- "Get access to a diverse set of analytics with Linkd"
- "Use custom domains when you use Linkd"

Observed issue: the copy describes the company and features directly, repeats the product name, and relies on generic claims rather than a sharp user outcome.

## Level 2 Pattern: Shorter and More Product-Specific

Representative examples:

- "The smarter way to share links"
- "Real-time click tracking, revenue attribution, bot filtering, and A/B routing"
- "Sign Up"

Observed improvement: the copy is shorter, the product value is clearer, and CTAs become more consistent.

## Level 3 Pattern: Stronger Feature Story

Representative examples:

- "Know exactly where every click goes"
- "Collect and analyze data in minutes not days"
- "Your links are working. Find out how."
- "Every link, unmistakably yours"
- "Control every single aspect of our links, to perfectly match your brand"

Observed improvement: copy becomes more confident and feature sections connect better to product visuals and proof elements.

## Level 4 Pattern: Outcome-Led and Polished

Representative examples:

- "Every click tells a story"
- "Real-time data that turns link activity into decisions"
- "Stop Guessing, Start Tracking"
- "From first click to final conversion, never miss a thing."
- "Every link, unmistakably yours"
- "Make your short link uniquely yours to build trust and raise click through rates"
- "Protect any page in a matter of seconds, with low latency deliverable routing"
- "Work seamlessly with your team, with workspaces, shared links, and permissions"

Observed improvement: the strongest copy translates product mechanics into business/user outcomes. It is concise enough for the visuals to carry part of the explanation.

## Navigation and Menu Copy

Representative examples:

- "Analytics" / "Turn data into decisions"
- "Customer Profiles" / "Learn about the people that matter"
- "Branding" / "Make you short links yours"
- "API Documentation" / "Connect to Linkd programmatically"
- "Changelog" / "Releases and updates"
- "Careers" / "Join our global, remote team"

Observed improvement: richer menu labels explain the destination, not just the category. This makes the product feel deeper and easier to explore.

## Copy Upgrade Rule

When improving a landing page, rewrite copy in this order:

1. Remove repeated product/company names when the context is already obvious.
2. Replace long descriptive paragraphs with one clear promise and one supporting sentence.
3. Change feature mechanics into customer outcomes.
4. Let product visuals handle concrete details where possible.
5. Keep CTA labels consistent when they lead to the same action.
